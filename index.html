  • POWERLESS: Issues 1-3
    in partnership with Voice of the City
    by Mitch Salm and David M. Brent
    Directed by Jack Tamburri


    June 28-August 20
    Voice of the City (www.voiceofthecity.org)
    3429 W. Diversey,
    Logan Square, Chicago


    The story of POWERLESS revolves around a group of young heroes in Logan Square who attempt to understand and develop their powers in a city where heroes are relegated to bureaucratic paper-pushing and crimes are solved by non-masked, non-caped, and non-super cops. Follow the antics of the rag tag SYS, government regulated MSCC (Metropolitan Superhero Conglomerate Corporation), and our protagonists, freshly graduated Milo, Massen and Maggie, as they attempt to determine the role of the hero in modern day Chicago's urban jungle.

The Society of Young Superheroes is an organization that exists to challenge the current superhero culture in Chicago. In a city where heroes are relegated to bureaucratic paper-pushing and crimes are solved by non-masked, non-super cops, the SYS is an order of crime-fighters who walk the streets instead of the office halls. Join us in our attempt to revive what it means to be a true “hero”!

As an indication of the SYS’s commitment to its community, we hold events in which our non-super neighbors and friends can participate. Please note that card-carrying members of the MSCC (Metropolitan Superhero Conglomerate Corporation) will not be admitted to these events. Check out our Goings On to see where you can find us next.

The Society of Young Superheroes is made possible with the assistance of our unbeatable allies. They've kindly lent their brains, their time and/or their dollars in order to foster the development of both the SYS and our production of POWERLESS: Issues 1-3. We would save these fine folks from a super villain any day.
